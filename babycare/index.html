Overview

BabyCare is a concept app created during my study at CareerFoundry, it is designed to help parents get quick and professional remote consultation with health specialists for their children.

Role & Responsibilities

Solo UX/UI Designer

• User Research
• Prototyping
• Usability Test
• Visual Design

My Design Process

Research

The Problem

How might we offer fast, reliable, professional answers to parents in a personal and reassuring environment? Parents need quick and trustworthy advice from health professionals based on their needs.

Product Goals

Parents are often overwhelmed by the amount of conflicting information regarding their children’s health.

Looking for advice online doesn’t always help; online forums are full of concerned parents looking for solutions and comments are from other concerned parents, instead of health experts.

  • Design an app with a video call feature that allows users to see which experts are online and available
  • For additional peace of mind, all experts will have a profile showing their area of expertise and qualifications.
  • A chat option will also be available to instantly chat with an online expert.
  • The app will have tailored advice from health professionals based on age and children’s information.

Competitive Analysis

I analysed competitors’ key objectives, marketing profile, overall strategy, created both SWOT and UX analyses.


Since there is a lack of remote consultation apps specifically for children’s health, I decided to look at one of the top apps in the UK for remote consultation for adult health called Babylon.


I also looked at one of the top apps for baby health tracking, featuring a community of parents asking questions and receiving answers called Ovia Parenting.

Insights

Considering children’s health is a very delicate topic, parents need to trust their resource(s) in order to be properly reassured.
The apps I researched lack transparency and the availability of children’s health specialists. There is currently no function for users to check health specialists’ profiles and leave ratings on specialists or the information received.

Survey

My research started with understanding users’ needs and attitudes.
I focused on what users want, specifically regarding the preferred way to contact an expert. I quickly realised that chat was a very important feature for users.

Interviews

I interviewed potential users, all participants have children between 0-2 years of age.

In the interviews, I learned that all users have a similar approach and a shared problem of trust when searching for answers online.
Despite this, users still primarily use Google for their research, although it’s of note that existing apps seem to be appreciated for their tailored advice.

Insights

  • All users seemed uncertain about the gravity of their own problems and it seems like they are looking for reassurance preferring a call and a more “human” contact.

 

  • The perfect app for them would be a 24/7 expert availability with trustworthy health experts with the possibility to chat and call immediately, plus a way to receive tailored notifications and advice based on children’s age.

User Journey

User Persona & Journey

Based on user research I identified two personas with different needs.
I created user journeys to help me empathise with potential users.

User Flow

I took a step back and concentrated on user flows in order to make my design more user-centered and, therefore, more successful from the users’ point of view.
I designed user flows for Seach and Save Expert, Booking a call and Start a Chat.

Insights

The results of the research phase made it clear that I was dealing with a problem related to trust as well as experts’ availability.

This data helped me focus during the design process giving priority to the Experts’ page, creating profiles with useful, detailed information and implementing a review system to help users feel reassured.

I wanted the user to feel confident and informed when choosing the right expert for them.

The problem of availability is a tricky one as professionals can’t be instantly available because of the way the booking system works.

This helped me think of a way to bypass this scheduling issue; although seeing and talking with an Expert is the most direct form of communication, it’s not the most convenient for busy Experts.

The Chat feature allows access to the Experts that are immediately available (controlled on the Experts’ end) also lets users choose whether they want to immediately start chatting or book an appointment for a video call.

Users also want to learn more about children’s health, which I wanted to make easily and quickly available.

The Baby page provides advice and articles written by health professionals that will automatically update based on the child’s info and age.

Ideate

Card Sorting & Sitemap

I asked participants to organise content topics into categories in order to give me an understanding of the most user-friendly way to organise my application.
Thanks to card sorting, I was able to refine my Sitemap accordingly.

Insights

That data informed an adjustment to the Dashboard.
With just four main categories (Dashboard, Search Expert, Call & Chat and My profile), the user experience is smoother and intuitive.
Call & Chat will have a list of all past chats and users will be able to start a new chat or book a previously contacted Expert again.
Search Expert allows the user to search for an Expert, book a call, or save to favourites. Settings will be under My Profile due to a relatively lower frequency of use.

Prototype

Wireframes & Prototype

After card sorting, I started sketching the core features and designing mid-fidelity wireframes.
The wireframes will be tested by users to identify pain points and optimise the design.

Test

Usability Test

I conducted Usability Test to assess the learnability of new users interacting with the BabyCare app for the first time. I observed and measured if users understand the app and how to complete functions such as logging in, searching experts, booking an appointment and chatting with experts.

Thanks to the Usability Test I was able to define and solve a few issues.

Issue #1

The Dashboard looks more clean and tidy by adjusting spacing and proportion.
The Search bar was enlarged and is the main point of focus, instead of the Symptoms Checker button.
The top-rated doctor section was enlarged in order to see the expert’s details and picture more clearly.

Issue #2

Some participants pointed out that the expert info on top during the booking process was distracting and not useful. Additionally, the credit system was confusing to users.
The credit system was entirely removed, along with the redundant information during the booking flow. After resizing the expert profile picture, the flow was streamlined.

Preference Test

After conducting a preference test with 16 users, 75% of them have expressed a preference for option #1, which is why new users will see Sign Up on top of Log In in the start screen.

Final Design

Learnings

  • Always keep in mind the problem that you are trying to solve.

 

  • Research and testing are both very important tools to make sure the design is created with intention.

 

  • Don’t be too attached to a design idea because that can change rapidly after user testing and make sure you are always prioritising user’s needs.

 

  • Feedback from peers and users it’s crucial and beneficial for both personal growth and design improvement.
