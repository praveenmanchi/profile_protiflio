Overview

LingoCards is a concept app created as my first project during my study at CareerFoundry, it is designed to help students who want to learn new languages providing them with a fast, user-friendly approach to enrich their vocabulary.

Role & Responsibilities

Solo UX/UI Designer

• User Research
• Prototyping
• Usability Test

My Design Process

Research

The Problem

Learning a language is time-consuming and there is a lot of vocabulary to remember.

Starting from grammar is not always the best way for busy students who have limited time juggling private life and classes.

We need an app that allows students to learn new vocabulary quickly without digging into the grammar and spending a lot of time.

Product Goals

Today’s time is limited, people struggle to have free time to dedicate to their passion or to learn new skills.

There is not always time to dive deep into the culture and grammar to learn new vocabulary, so we need an app that matches the following goals:

  • Allow students to learn new languages through flashcards.
  • Ability to talk to locals to improve their language and communication.
  • Evaluate users skills thanks to a test at the end of each section.

Competitive Analysis

I analysed competitors’ key objectives, marketing profile, overall strategy, created both SWOT and UX analyses.
I analysed two apps called Quizlet and Tinycards, they are both flashcards apps that have games to make learning more fun.

Insights

  • The apps analysed focus on just one way to learn a language and doesn’t allow students to vary their study methods.
  • Users cannot speak to someone to test their language skills but just learn vocabulary by repetition.
  • Users decks are not always reliable.

Interviews

I interviewed three potential users, all participants are using apps to learn a new language.

In the interviews, I learned that all users were using Duolingo for learning a new language.

Insights

  • All users use the app for a limited time during the day.
  • Users need an app they can use quickly with theory and real like scenarios.
  • Rewarding could help to keep users entertained.

User Journey

Proto-Persona

Based on user research I identified a proto-persona.

User Flow

I took a step back and concentrated on user flows in order to make my design more user-centred and, therefore, more successful from the users’ point of view.
I designed user flows for Add a word to collection and Book a call with a local.

Insights

The results of the research phase made it clear that I was dealing with a problem related to time and real-life knowledge when it comes to languages.
I decided to approach the problems by adding features that may make users lives easier.

Using flashcards will improve the number of times users need to learn new words, instead of having to learn grammar or use a more traditional method, at the same time I wanted to solve the problem of being ready to use that language when in real-life scenarios.

I added video flashcards with mother-tongues to give a more authentic feel and make sure users will also learn how to pronounce words correctly, this feature by itself didn’t feel enough to solve the problem in its entirety, I then decided to add the feature to video call locals and speak about certain topics, in this way users are not overwhelmed and they will feel confident to speak in real-life scenarios.

Test

Usability Test

I conducted Usability Test to assess the learnability of new users interacting with the app for the first time.
I observed and measured if users understand the app and how to complete functions.

Thanks to the Usability Test I was able to define and solve a few issues.

  • Some users found it difficult to search for locals with the same interests, I added a featured section in the search flow, where users can find locals with interests in common.
  • A small number of users needed to watch video flashcards more than once to understand the word correctly, I added the option of slowing down the video to facilitate the learning.

Learnings

  • Being the first project, I learned a lot about the design thinking process.

 

  • Research and interviews helped me going in the right direction.

 

  • I learned to not get attached too much to a design and always prioritise users’ feedback.
